So What is Ad Blocking?

While sometimes called ad filtering, Ad Blocking is the most commonly used term. Ad blocking software restricts advertisements from showing when you surf the web, stream content, and play online games. This software is usually installed as a browser extention but there are also external software programs and hardware available.

Some of the reasons for blocking ads include reducing the amount of ads shown while online, skipping ads during streaming content and to protect one's privacy. Since there are less ads being shown the number of cookies collected is reduced. According to Phillip Elmer-Dewitt, as of 2015, 45 million Americans were using ad blockers. While online advertisement and ad blocking software are contentious issues, there are arguments both for and against the practice of ad blocking. There have been multiple lawsuits claiming that ad blockers are illegal becsause they prevent marketing companies from doing their job, and therefore there is no fair and competetive market. Those on the other side believe they should have the right to select what content is allowed on their network. As with most new technology, it will take litigation in order to decide how the regulation of ads can be done.

So how does ad blocking work? Ad blockers filter media types when a page is loaded or an app is opened. Most extension ad blockers stop flash animation and image loading so that banner ads don't show when the page loads. Some programs block known advertisement sites through DNS filtering and some Virtual Private Networks (VPN) have ad blocking capabilities.
