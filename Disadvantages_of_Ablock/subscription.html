Subscribe to the Internet

Ad block overused?

32% of the web uses some sort of ad block, because being bombarded by a bunch of ads while searching for information, or for entertainment is frustrating. While a lot of us do know that it hinders the growth of the website, we just assume the next person that visits the site won't have ad block and the site will get there revenue then, but if it were reversed, I know I wouldn't provide a resource without some sort of return. In order for these sites to continue to grow, they have to become more aggressive on how to either influence people to turn off ad block, or require them to pay a fee to view there content. Which is why google came out with Google Contributor, to help content creators while allowing people to combat advertisements.

Google Contributor

Google Contributor is basically a monthly subscription to google who divides up your subscription to your most visited cites. So that way you can support the content you rely on the most, whether it be a news station, or just a blogger you enjoy reading. As we gain more of our entertainment and information from the web, it is a natural evolution that advertisements and subscriptions (such as cable/dish) would follow.

Conclusion

Is google contributor the right way to go? In all it was just a matter of time, with Hulu, Netflix, Twitch, etc. All based on there subscription business instead of ads, unless your on those sights for free content. With google trying to take a lead on this to help out smaller cites, or smaller followings, it opens ups a new door way for content creators to suggest to there viewers to support them. Specially since, a lot of people prefer helping the individual they enjoy.
