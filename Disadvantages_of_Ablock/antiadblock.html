Ad Blocking Technologies

Ad blockers were born around 2002 as a response to intrusive advertising and to sites that used malware or spyware.

Ad blockers work in many different ways.

One method is simply to block (or prevent auto-play of) video and audio files. This can be done in most browsers easily. This method is refined by numerous browser extensions.

The more advanced ad blocking filter software allow better control of advertisements through advanced features, such as blacklists and whitelists filters. Many modern antivirus software also include ad blocker functionality.

Popular Ad Blocking Technologies

Here is the list of the most popular technologies in 2019:

Browser Integration
As of 2015, many web browsers block unsolicited pop-up ads automatically. Browser extensions, such as AdBlock, Adblock Plus, and uBlock Origin, provide regularly updated filter lists. Google released its built-in ad blocker for the Chrome/Chromium in February 2018, which blocks some ads by default.
External Programs
A number of external software applications offer ad filtering as a primary or additional feature. A traditional solution is to customize an HTTP proxy (or web proxy) to filter content. These programs work by caching and filtering content before it is displayed in a user's browser. This provides an opportunity to remove not only ads but also content which may be offensive, inappropriate, or even malicious.
Other popular methods include:
-Hosts file and DNS manipulation
-DNS filtering
-Recursive Local VPN
-Hardware devices

From Ad Blocking To Better Life

According to 5 Tips for Getting the Most Out of Online Advertising, the average person sees more than 5000 advertisements daily. Regardless of the technology you decide to use for ad blocking, fewer ads will contribute to your quality of life, faster page loads and monetary savings from avoiding unnecessary impulse purchases.
